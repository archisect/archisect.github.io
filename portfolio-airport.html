Pier Concepts looks to be most suitable for this terminal building. As the building has 11 Gates, Linear concept will increase the concourse size and by dividing aircrafts to both sides the length of concourse can be kept short. One pier will be enough as the gates are not too much in number

DESIGN INFLUENCE

Building is South facing and hence will receive heat during summers. To reduce the effect of heat, roof is extended about 30 m in front. This will let the road and curbs area to be under shadow. At the north the curtain wall provided at the sides lets the north light inside the building. Heat from the west side will be reduced by the botanical park provided in this direction at International Departures. Trussed roof is designed for the terminal. This will lead to larger free space at 3rd floor which will increase aesthetics of the building. The roof is curved and north light ventilations are provided.

PLANS

Columns are placed in decreasing order from bottom to top. This leads to more free space at upper floors where passenger flow will be most. Column at ground are 7.5m center to center. At 1st floor alternative columns are removed. The column sizes are 700mm, 1200mm and 1600mm. 700mm column will stop at the ground floor while 1200mm column will continue till 2nd floor and 1600mm column will continue to 3rd floor and will support trussed roof.

VIEWS

The proposed new International airport shall be capable of handling one B-747 one AB-300 aircraft load corresponding to a peak hour of 600 departing and 600 arriving passengers. The program outlined for the terminal has been designed to respond to both more immediate and longer-term needs anticipated for the airport. The aviation linked commercial development has been evolved around airport including Basic Amenities , shopping plaza and office parks, hospitality industry, promotional activities of tourism, logistic park and housing.
